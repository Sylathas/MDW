Hydro Lighting Surface is a membrane covered in an epoxy resin coating, applied by silk-screen, by making opposites functions such as solid/flexible and hydrophilic/hydrophobic able to be inscribed in a fabric. The adhesion properties change in such a way that drops of water accumulate on the printed structures, triggering further functions such as hydrochromism and thermochromism. Fluorescent pigments make the textile glow in the dark, which is further enhanced by the light refraction of the water drops. The glow-effect is triggered by the interaction with water: Wherever the conductive drops accumulate, an LED circuit is temporarily completed. Use in high-visibility clothing and façade construction is conceivable.

Light and matter are typically considered separately, but what happens when light comes directly from matter? Imagine buildings that are able to light up whenever it rains and crosswalks that gently glow in the dark for better visibility.

LED technology is fundamental for this kind of material. The manufacturing cost of LEDs is declining primarily because of the decrease of chips and components used in the manufacturing process. This could heavily impact the sustainability of this material and its uses in all fields.

New materials equal new possibilities in the fashion world, from transparent to self-repairing all the way to thermochromic fabrics.
